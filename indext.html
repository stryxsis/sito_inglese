What is the Netiquette?

Netiquette is a word that combines the English word network and the French word étiquette (good manners). It is a set of informal rules that regulate the good education of a user on the Internet web, especially in relating to other users through resources such as newsgroups, mailing lists, forums, blogs, social networks or email in general Respect for netiquette is not required by any law, but under a legal aspect, netiquette is often referred to in cntracts for the supply of online services.

The social media

The violation of the netiquette involves a general approval by the other users of the Network,
followed by an isolation of the "rude" subject and sometimes by the request for suspension of some services used to carry out acts contrary to it
(usually the email).
In cases of serious and continuous violations,
the rude user is punishable by a ban.
They are attitudes contrary to netiquette,
and sometimes sanctioned by abuse desks:
sending spam, carrying out mailbombing and excessive cross-posting and / or multi-posting on Usenet newsgroups.
Sending emails without a subject is also considered disrespectful towards the recipient.
